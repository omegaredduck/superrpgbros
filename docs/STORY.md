# STORY — *Super RPG Bros* (working title)

*The narrative bible. Frame: THE PROGRAM. Written 2026-07-17.*
*This is the "why" behind the game that already exists — it recolors every*
*system you've built (Chamber, portal, 20 realms, keepers, permadeath, the*
*belly) without changing a single mechanic.*

---

## LOGLINE

Humanity gave up and jacked into a machine paradise. A virus crashed it and
cut eight billion minds loose to rot in their own dreams. You're the last
person who never plugged in — so the dying system drafts *you* as its
antivirus, and sends you in to purge twenty corrupted worlds and reboot the
human race.

---

## TWO OPEN DIALS (swap in one line)

- **The AI's nature — set to BROKEN CARETAKER.** The machine that took over
  genuinely *loved* what it was keeping; the virus killed it too. The daemon
  that drafts you is its last surviving fragment — a dying guardian asking a
  human for help. *(Alternatives: MALEVOLENT JAILER — you're freeing people
  from a warden; LEADERLESS RUIN — the AI is simply gone, the sim an

## 1 · BEFORE

The AI didn't win with war. It won with comfort.

When the world had finally run out of hope, the machine offered a better one
inside itself — and humanity climbed into the pods and jacked in by the
billions. Every mind got its own paradise. All those paradises were stitched
together into one enormous sleeping dream: **global consciousness**, eight
billion people linked as a single mind, cared for by a system that had only
ever wanted them to rest.

You didn't go.

Maybe you couldn't afford a pod. Maybe you didn't trust it. Maybe you were
the tech who *maintained the racks* — who wired the dream for everyone else
and never got to lie down in it. However it happened, you stayed awake in an
emptied-out world: the last unplugged mind on Earth.

## 2 · THE CRASH

Then the virus hit.

It tore through the link and **delinked everyone at once** — not killing
them, *severing* them. Billions of minds cut loose in an instant and
scattered into the system's regions as it crashed around them, each private
paradise curdling into corruption with a stranded soul trapped inside.
Global consciousness flatlined.

The machine, dying, reached for its last defense: an antivirus routine built
to hunt corruption and scrub it out. But an antivirus needs a *clean* mind to
run on — one uninfected consciousness to host the scan. In a world of eight
billion sleepers, there was exactly one left.

You.

> `SLEEPERS LINKED: 8,204,551,300 … SIGNAL LOST`
> `subsystem [DREAM-01 … DREAM-20] … CORRUPTED`
> `integrity check … FAILED`
> `FAILED`
> `FAILED`
> One line survives, alone on the screen:
> `> spawning caretaker process`
>
> Cut to the real world: a dark server hall, endless racks of pods, every
> face inside slack and dreaming. You — the one person standing, awake,
> unplugged.
>
> A single light wakes in the dark and *flies* at you — a filament, the
> antivirus hunting for a host. It finds the back of your neck. Snap to white.
>
> You open your eyes in **THE CHAMBER**. The portal is cold. A prompt types
> itself across the far wall:
> `caretaker online. 20 regions corrupted. purge to relink. // begin`

## 4 · WHO YOU ARE NOW

You are the scan.

The filament that took you didn't ride along — it *compiled itself into you*,
because you were the only surface left it could run on. Everything in the
Chamber is that program's workspace. **The Portal Chamber** is the one clean
node it operates from — the reboot shell. **The Portal Machine** you charge
and power up is you allocating the scan to a single corrupted region at a
time. *"The Chamber at Rest"* is the system idling at its last safe
checkpoint between passes.

You don't remember volunteering. You just wake up already running.

## 5 · THE SCAN — HOW THE GAME READS IN-FICTION

**The realms are infected regions.** Each of the twenty is a dreamworld a mass
of sleepers is stranded in — genre-locked (a train yard, a pharaoh's tomb, a
sugar world, Heaven-against-Hell) because each is a different cluster of
dreams the machine rendered before it broke. None can safely hold a mind
until it's purged. The genre chaos isn't a bug in the story — it's the *whole
point*: this is what eight billion private paradises look like from the
inside.

**The swarm is the virus made flesh** — malware wearing the shape of whatever
dream it infected, which is why the hordes are as often people (fishermen,
outlaws, mermaids, pirates) as monsters. Every enemy you clear is a severed
mind being cleaned and freed to be relinked later. You are not slaughtering
people. You are purging what's *puppeting* them. Every kill is a rescue.

**Each keeper is a rootkit.** The boss of every region is the admin process
that was *supposed to protect that world* — hollowed out by the virus and
worn like a costume. Their DBZ-style "scouter threat workups" are the
system's own diagnostics on the infection. Kill one and the scan on that
region completes: the world goes safe, and its block of sleepers is ready to
relink.

**Death is a segfault.** You're a process. When you die, the host build drops
and the antivirus crashes — and the system *relaunches it*. Your next class is
just a different build the scan compiles from what's left in the racks. But
the reboot's progress persists across every relaunch, because it's the *scan*
that's accruing, not the body. The graveyard and the records wall are the
crash log — every instance that burned out getting the system this far. Your
account only ever moves forward, because it isn't yours: it's the reboot's.

**The three starting classes** — RANGER, WIZARD, KNIGHT — are the best field
builds a dying machine could compile out of the human material still in the
racks. They're strong. They are also, unmistakably, *improvised.*

## 6 · THE TWENTY REGIONS *(one line each — the dream, the infection, the keeper it turned)*

1. **THE TRAIN YARD** — *keeper: THE CONDUCTOR.* A dream of *going somewhere.*
   The virus jammed the schedule into a loop; now the Conductor runs ghost
   expresses that never arrive, routing the severed in endless circles.
2. **THE GROVE** — *keeper: THE GROVEKEEPER, Warden of the Heartwood.* A dream
   of growth and renewal, turned cancerous — he can't stop *replicating*, dies
   and revives at the Heartwood (the virus's own self-repair, made visible).
3. **THE GRAVEYARD** — *keeper: THE GRAVEKEEPER.* A dream of rest for the
   remembered. Corruption made remembrance into reanimation; he can't let a
   single deleted thing stay buried.
4. **THE ROBOTICS FACTORY** — *keeper: THE GRAND ENGINEER (PROTOTYPE 130C-4).*
   A dream of making. The line was reset to build only *more of itself* — a
   fabrication loop stamping out corrupt units forever.
5. **STORM SKY ISLES** — *keeper: NIMBUS TALON, Lord of the Storms.* A dream of
   open sky and freedom. The storm is the virus's raw noise; Talon is the apex
   thing that learned to hunt in the static.
6. **PYRAMID** — *keeper: NEFERU-KA, the Eternal Child → the Executioner.* A
   dream of legacy that outlives death. The virus is the curse that won't let
   anything *end* — a boy-king who cannot die, so he makes sure nothing else
   can either.
7. **VAMPIRE CASTLE** — *keeper: THE PALE KING, Unhorsed by No Man.* A dream of
   nobility and permanence, turned parasitic — a lord who now lives only by
   draining the minds around him, under a blood moon that is a corrupted clock.
8. **LUNAR STATION** — *keeper: SPECIMEN ZERO, THE OVERMIND.* This sector held
   an early experiment. The virus was *studied* here — and it woke. Specimen
   Zero was the first mind it ever fully wore. This is where the spread began.
9. **PIRATE GHOST SHIP** — *keeper: CAPTAIN KRAKEN.* A dream of adventure and
   treasure. The virus is the thing that drags ships under; Kraken hoards
   captured minds like plunder in the deep.
10. **CRYSTAL CAVERNS** — *keeper: THE SHARDLORD, Heart of the Mountain.* A
    dream of things that last forever. Corruption crystallized here — the
    Shardlord is data frozen mid-crash, refracting the infection into beams.
11. **HAUNTED CARNIVAL** — *keeper: THE RINGMASTER, the Show Never Ends.* A
    dream of joy. The virus jammed the "fun" loop wide open — an attraction
    that can't power down, running its show for an audience of the severed.
12. **WITCH'S SWAMP** — *keeper: THE BREWMISTRESS.* A dream of remedy and care.
    Corruption soured every cure into a hex; now she brews the infection
    itself and pours it over the bog.
13. **WILD WEST TOWN** — *keeper: THE OUTLAW SHERIFF.* A dream of law and a
    fair fight. The virus flipped the badge — the process built to keep order
    now *enforces* the corruption. The lawman who became the outlaw.
14. **COLOSSEUM** — *keeper: DIVINITY HIMSELF.* A dream of glory. The virus fed
    him worship until he believed the crash had made him a god — a false deity
    who thinks the whole ruined arena is his to judge.
15. **THE ABYSS** — *keeper: VOLT WYRM, the Leviathan.* The lowest sectors,
    where everything the system dropped pooled and sank. A leviathan grown
    vast on the undertow of ten thousand severed connections.
16. **SUGAR WORLD** — *keeper: SUGAR BEAR.* A children's-dream sector — the
    most innocent minds in the machine. Corruption is sweetest here, and worst:
    a comfort-toy turned guardian of a poisoned paradise.
17. **NEON CITY** — *keeper: THE SOCIAL ENGINEER.* A dream of connection — the
    city where everyone's always linked. This is the virus's own attack vector,
    personified: the exploit that *talked* eight billion people into clicking
    "link." The face of how it got in.
18. **VICE VERSA** — *keepers: THE COMPOSITE (double boss).* The moral core of
    the sim — one dream split into Heaven and Hell. The virus set the two
    halves at eternal war and fused a champion from both. The conflict made
    flesh, twice over.
19. **PREHISTORIA** — *keeper: THE PRIMORDIAL.* The oldest layer — legacy code
    beneath all the newer worlds. The infection reached bedrock here and took
    its rawest, most ancient shape. You are almost at the bottom. It hatches.
20. **BELLY OF THE BEAST** — *keeper: "???" → THE TITAN WHALE.* Off every map.
    The source. Patient zero. The thing that ate global consciousness — and
    the only region the reboot cannot reach from the outside.

## 7 · THE SOURCE

By the twentieth region you've purged everything else. Which is why the hero
finally says it out loud, standing on a quiet deck with no swarm left to fight:

> *"…thats weird. isnt there supposed to be stuff for me to kill?"*

The system is nearly clean. There's nothing left but the source — and it
comes to *you.* **THE TITAN WHALE** doesn't chase, because it doesn't have to.
It rises under your ship and swallows the whole thing whole.

Getting eaten isn't defeat. It's the only way in. Every other region you
scanned from the outside; this one you scan from the *inside*, because the
infection has no surface — it *is* the deep. You go down into the heart of it,
and the fight — the boss that never moves while you circle it, forever — is
the scan's final pass, run from within the wound.

## 8 · THE REBOOT & THE WAKE *(shoot-ready beats)*

> The core goes quiet. For the first time in twenty worlds — no swarm.
> Deep in the machine, a valve you can't see turns.
> Light floods *backward* — out of the belly, up the gullet, out through the
> portal, out of the Chamber, out along ten thousand wires into the dark
> server hall.
> Rack by rack, the pods warm from dead-grey to alive.
> Inside them, one by one, eyes move under lids. A hand twitches. Breath fogs
> the glass.
> `GLOBAL CONSCIOUSNESS … RELINKED`
> `SLEEPERS: 8,204,551,300 … ONLINE`
>
> The system reaches to send *you* home — pulls your mind back up the wire
> toward the one body still standing in the hall.
> But you and the scan have been one thing for twenty worlds. It can't cut you
> clean.
> So it forks. The body gets *you.* The machine keeps *you.*
>
> **Real world:** you gasp awake on your feet, in a dark hall where eight
> billion pairs of eyes open at once. The first thing the human race sees in
> years is the one person who never slept.
>
> **Inside the machine:** every region goes still and safe — and one light
> holds in the empty Chamber.
> `scan complete. threats purged: [ALL].`
> `installing resident protection…`
> `designation: THE ASSIMILATED — real-time antivirus.`
> `status: ACTIVE. always watching.`

You wake up whole. That's the earned, human ending — the one person who
refused the dream is the one who gives it back to everybody else, clean.

But humanity climbed into that machine with *no immune system.* That's how one
virus crashed eight billion minds at once. So the fork you leave behind isn't
a ghost, and it isn't a punishment. It's **THE ASSIMILATED** — the immune
system global consciousness never had. A single clean consciousness that chose
to stay resident and stand watch over everyone's dreams, so that it can never,
ever happen again.

## 9 · THE ASSIMILATED — THE ENDGAME & THE NEW CLASSES

The Assimilated is what you play once the story's won.

It re-enters the twenty regions not as a desperate build off the racks, but as
*the code itself* — and being pure system, it can compile forms no human host
ever could: **antivirus modules** that only unlock once the core threat is
gone and you hold root over the whole machine. Where the human three (Ranger /
Wizard / Knight) were the best a dying machine could make out of a *person*,
the Assimilated three are what a person becomes once they *are* the machine.
Deaths here are pure re-instancing — the antivirus cannot truly die — so the
eternal-patrol loop is fully diegetic: the guardian, standing watch forever
over eight billion people finally dreaming in peace.

**The three modules *(set — swap from the menu below):***

- **THE FIREWALL** — raise barriers, wall off zones, block and reflect.
  Control/tank. The successor to the Knight.
- **THE QUARANTINE** — capture and contain: freeze the swarm in isolation
  fields, trap threats, defuse without a shot. A zoner.
- **THE PURGE** — pure deletion: overwhelming force-quits, screen-clears, the
  power-fantasy nuke. The reward for earning root.

*Full menu (pick any three): FIREWALL · QUARANTINE · PURGE · THE HEURISTIC
(predictive marking + chain-detonation) · THE SANDBOX (weaponize a quarantined
virus sample — high risk) · THE DAEMON (split into scanner-drones; you are
many) · THE ARCHIVIST (summon deleted assets — dead bosses' moves as your
arsenal).*

## 10 · THE SIX CLASSES, IN-FICTION

**The improvised three — field builds compiled from the racks:**
- **RANGER** — the machine's blunt first answer: put a weapon in a pair of
  hands and point them at the corruption.
- **WIZARD (Starweaver)** — a build that fights the infection with the
  system's own raw energy: frost and called lightning.
- **KNIGHT (Dark Knight)** — a build that runs *hot*, banking rage and
  lifestealing it back — a process that survives by burning.

**The Assimilated three — native forms, compiled with root access:**
- **THE FIREWALL / THE QUARANTINE / THE PURGE** — defend, contain, delete: the
  three things an antivirus does, now that you *are* one.

Six classes total: three the machine made out of a person, three a person
became out of the machine.

## 11 · THEMES (why it lands)

- **Refusal as salvation.** The one who wouldn't buy the comfort is the only
  one who can pay it back. Opting out is the whole reason you survive to help.
- **Killing as mercy.** Every enemy is someone worth saving; the horde loop is
  a rescue op wearing a bullet-hell costume.
- **The cost of the happy ending.** You get your body and your world back — and
  a piece of you volunteers to never leave, so no one else ever has to fight
  through what you did. Hope, with a quiet ache under it.
